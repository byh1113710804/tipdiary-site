Switch from ServerLife to TipDiary

Want to move your tip history from ServerLife to TipDiary? People usually switch because they want local-first storage with no account — your data stays on your device. Here's how to bring your shifts with you.

Honest heads-up: TipDiary imports a generic CSV file and lets you map the columns yourself. There's no one-click ServerLife import, so the steps below use a plain spreadsheet export — which keeps working even if ServerLife rearranges its menus.
1

Export your data from ServerLife

Open ServerLife and look for an Export option in its settings or share menu. Most tip trackers can email you a spreadsheet or send your shift log to another app. Export your full history and send it somewhere you can open on a computer or in a spreadsheet app (email it to yourself, or save it to Files/Drive).

2

Save it as a CSV file

If you received a spreadsheet or an email attachment, open it in any spreadsheet app — Apple Numbers, Microsoft Excel, or Google Sheets — and choose Export / Save As → CSV. If ServerLife already gave you a .csv file, you can skip this step.

3

Match your columns to TipDiary's

TipDiary reads these columns. You don't have to rename anything in advance — during import you'll map each of your columns to one of these fields:

date, job, cash_tips, card_tips, tip_in, tip_out, hours, sales, note
ColumnWhat goes in it
dateShift date (e.g. 2026-07-04 or 7/4/2026)
jobJob or employer name
cash_tipsCash tips for the shift
card_tipsCredit/debit card tips
tip_inTips you received from a tip pool (tip-in)
tip_outTips you paid out to others (tip-out)
hoursHours worked
salesYour total sales for the shift (optional)
noteAnything else you want to remember (optional)
4

Import into TipDiary

In TipDiary, go to Settings → Import CSV, pick your file, then map each column to the matching TipDiary field. Check the preview so amounts and dates look right, then confirm the import. Your shifts land in your history, and the No Tax on Tips math updates automatically.

Tips for a clean import: keep one row per shift; enter tip and sales amounts as plain numbers (no $ needed); and use a consistent date format. You can always edit or delete individual shifts afterward.
